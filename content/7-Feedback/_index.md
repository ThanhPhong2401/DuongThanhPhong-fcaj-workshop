### General Overview

**1. Learning Environment**

The learning environment here is approachable, open, and friendly, which has filled me with positive energy. The admins are very easygoing and always ready to enthusiastically help me and others resolve any questions regarding our studies and hands-on practice with new AWS services. Through the events organized to help us understand the field and the real-world impact of AWS services, I have come to highly appreciate the level of organization and the spirit of learning fostered in this environment.

**2. Mentorship and Community Support**

The support from the mentors and the AWS Study Group community is truly a valuable asset. Instead of providing direct answers, the mentors skillfully guided me to think critically and find solutions on my own. This approach has helped me develop logical thinking and become more resilient when facing real-world challenges, from configuring AWS services to managing build processes with Hugo.

**3. Alignment with Professional Goals**

As a Software Engineering student, I have found that this AWS program has broadened my perspective, shifting from purely building applications to operating them within the cloud computing landscape. The Task Management System project served as a bridge, allowing me to apply my programming mindset to cloud infrastructure design, security implementation, and system monitoring.

**4. Personal Development Opportunities**

This internship has helped me cultivate and master several practical skills:
* Creating intuitive and easy-to-understand technical workshop content and guides.
* Translating and summarizing technical blog posts.
* Enhancing self-learning abilities and the habit of proactively researching documentation and experimenting with new solutions before seeking help.
* Managing a personal website via Git, GitHub Actions, and GitHub Pages.
* Proactively troubleshooting build and system deployment errors.
* Refining time management skills to ensure reporting deadlines are consistently met.

**5. Community Culture**

"Tinh thần máu lửa được thắp lên tại Cộng đồng FCAJ là một thứ không thể thiếu khi nhắc tới văn hóa" (The passionate spirit ignited within the FCAJ community is an essential part of our culture). The environment here is not just for technical exchange; it is a place that bonds individuals with shared passions for learning, creating, and exploring the world of AWS. Everyone’s enthusiasm helped me overcome any sense of being in an unfamiliar space, turning intense working hours into inspiring learning experiences.

### Most Satisfying Experience
The most satisfying experience I have had here is the willingness of senior members to share their knowledge, experience, and insights. They are always ready to spend time answering every small question from interns like us. Because of this, we no longer hesitate to ask questions, which has made our learning journey truly meaningful and worth every second.

### Suggestions for Improvement
* Create a repository of exemplary reports from previous cohorts to provide interns with a clear reference for optimizing the internship process.
* Host in-depth troubleshooting workshops to quickly resolve common technical issues with Hugo and GitHub Actions.
* Provide a checklist of content requirements for each reporting stage to help interns organize their work systematically and avoid mistakes.
* Increase architecture review sessions so that interns can receive direct feedback from the community and enhance their design thinking.
* Provide more detailed guidance on cost estimation methods and security optimization for AWS projects.

### Spreading the Word
Having gained so many meaningful experiences during my internship here, I will definitely recommend this program to other students, as it is truly an ideal place to start with AWS in a practical way. Even after my internship concludes, I will stay connected with the AWS Study Group to keep learning from everyone and continue growing together with this community.
